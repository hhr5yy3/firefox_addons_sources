You’re almost ready to start generating virtual card numbers with Eno from Capital One®!

To begin, please review the following information:

  • We collect technical and device information in the form of a digital fingerprint in order to protect your account from unauthorized access and to protect Capital One’s infrastructure from malicious activity and fraud.
  • When you use Eno to generate or retrieve existing virtual card numbers, Eno will communicate with Capital One over an encrypted connection. This communication requires storing and retrieving Capital One session cookies.
  • Part of the added security that virtual card numbers have over your actual card number is provided by merchant binding. In order to properly bind your virtual card numbers to a specific merchant, Eno collects the top-level domain of the site you are on each time you request a virtual card number. Rest assured that Eno is not collecting your entire web browsing history.
  • You may disable Eno on any site by using the popup control feature within the Settings menu.
  • To make the product better for you and other users and to mitigate fraud, we ask that you anonymously share data on which features you use. This includes data such as when you log in or out, change settings, where you might get stuck during different user experiences, or use product features like express checkout and popup control.
  • If you choose not to share anonymous feature usage data with Capital One, it may take Capital One longer to find and fix bugs.
  • View our privacy policy for additional information.
